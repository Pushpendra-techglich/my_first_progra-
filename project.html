There are multiple matches for Mahakala, including a deity in Hinduism and Buddhism and the Mahakaleshwar temple in Ujjain, India: Deity Mahakala is a deity in both Hinduism and Buddhism. In Hinduism, Mahakala is a fierce manifestation of Shiva, the Hindu god, and the consort of the goddess Mahākālī. In Buddhism, Mahakala is a wrathful manifestation of a Buddha and a protector deity. Temple The Mahakaleshwar temple in Ujjain, India is one of the 12 Jyotirlingas in the country. The temple is known for its imposing facade and the swayambhu (self-manifested) lingam that faces south. History The temple was built in the Bhumija style of architecture and was rebuilt in the 11th and 12th centuries, and again in the 18th century. Significance: The temple is said to be one of the most sacred abodes of Shiva and is believed to absolve all sins. Location: The temple is located 2 kilometers from the Ujjain Railway Station and about an hour and 10 minutes from the Indore Airport.

About temple

Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga (IPA: [mahākāleśvara]) is a Hindu temple dedicated to Shiva and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, shrines which are said to be the most sacred abodes of Shiva. It is located in the ancient city of Ujjain in the state of Madhya Pradesh, India.
